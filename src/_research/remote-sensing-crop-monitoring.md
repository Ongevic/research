During my stay at Aarhus University, I spent a lot of time on the messy but interesting side of crop research: field campaigns, agronomic measurements, chlorophyll observations, leaf area index, biomass, soil sampling, and the work of making these pieces talk to each other.

One part I especially liked was linking phenology and remote sensing data with crop models for wheat growth and disease monitoring, including yellow rust, powdery mildew, and Septoria.

I like this kind of project because it sits right at the boundary between field evidence, model calibration, and decision support.
